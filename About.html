Original Broadcast Date

October 16, 2001

Genres

Action/Adventure, Sci-Fi/Fantasy

Creators

Created by Alfred Gough and Miles Millar, the series ran for ten seasons and starred Tom Welling as Clark Kent alongside Kristin Kreuk as Lana Lang. Following their success with Netflix's Wednesday, Alfred Gough and Miles Millar look back on Smallville, proud of the story they told.

Cast

The Smallville cast featured Tom Welling as Clark Kent, exploring his journey to becoming Superman, and Michael Rosenbaum as Lex Luthor, Clark's friend-turned-enemy. Kristin Kreuk played Lana Lang, Clark's first love, while Allison Mack starred as Chloe Sullivan, his tech-savvy friend. The show also introduced Erica Durance as Lois Lane and Justin Hartley as Green Arrow in later seasons, expanding its DC universe.

Themes

The series explores Clark's struggle between his human upbringing and Kryptonian legacy. Themes of power, destiny, and morality are highlighted, with Kryptonite symbolizing Clark's vulnerabilities and Lex Luthor representing the dark side of ambition.

Legacy

Smallville reshaped superhero TV, influencing shows like Arrow and The Flash. Its take on Clark Kent's early years set a new standard for character development, making Superman's journey relatable to a new generation.
